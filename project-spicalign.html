SPICALIGN —
Pediatric Medical Device

An intraoperative positioning device designed to correctly orient pediatric hip spica casts for car-seat compatibility — developed in collaboration with Lurie Children's Hospital at the Segal Design Institute.

Mechanical Design Medical Device Prototyping User Research Lurie Children's Hospital
🏥 Drop spicalign.jpg here to add a photo

The Problem

Background

When young children receive hip spica casts for conditions like hip dysplasia or femur fractures, the cast must be carefully positioned to allow safe use of a car seat — a critical safety requirement for discharge. Currently, surgeons and nurses estimate this positioning by eye, which can lead to misalignment and the need for cast revisions.

SPICALIGN provides a mechanical solution: a device that holds the cast in the correct orientation during the casting process itself, removing the guesswork.

The Device

Mechanical System

The device uses a compact actuation system to position and hold a child's legs at the correct angles during casting. Key requirements included adjustability across patient sizes, ease of use in an OR environment, and sterility compatibility.

ComponentRole
Linear ActuatorPrimary height/position adjustment for accommodating different patient sizes
Servo MotorsControlled angular positioning of leg supports
Telescoping ArmsAdjustable reach to accommodate varied patient anatomy
Foot-Pedal ControlHands-free operation for surgeons working in a sterile field

Process

Design & Research

  • Conducted user interviews with pediatric nurses and certified car-seat technicians to define requirements
  • Collaborated directly with Lurie Children's Hospital clinicians throughout prototyping and testing
  • Iterated through multiple prototype generations based on clinical feedback
  • Documented design rationale and testing results per medical device development standards
