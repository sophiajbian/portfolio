Overview

The Project

Working as an undergraduate research assistant under Dr. Taewan Park at the Querrey Simpson Institute for Bioelectronics, I contribute to the development of a compact, wireless fluorimeter designed for continuous fluorescence sensing in biomedical research settings.

Fluorimeters measure the intensity of fluorescent light emitted by a sample — useful in applications like monitoring biological markers, drug delivery, or metabolic activity. The goal of this device is to make that sensing portable, wearable, and wireless.

Hardware

The Device

The prototype uses flexible PCB construction to allow conformable form factors suited to wearable or implantable biomedical applications. I've been involved in assembly, integration, and iterative testing of the hardware.
